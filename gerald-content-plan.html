Reflection
Review

Double down on the content type that performed. The Lone Wolf article and contrarian posts typically win. Month 2 = go deeper on what worked.
→ Report to The Signal
A note on voice
Every post should sound like something Gerald would say out loud in a retreat — not something a marketing team wrote. If it reads corporate, cut it. The power is in the specificity, the poetry, and the genuine depth. Jargon-free. Soul-full.
What not to do
Don't pitch. Don't announce events without telling a story first. Don't use "swipe left" or carousel hooks — that's not Gerald's voice. Don't post more than once per day. Quality and depth will always outperform volume here.
Images that work
Real moments from real retreats. Faces. Community. Circles of people in sacred spaces. Not stock photos. Not perfectly lit product shots. The rawness and authenticity of the real thing is the content.
The compound effect
Month 1 = planting. Month 2 = growing. Month 3+ = harvesting. LinkedIn compounds. Every post builds on the last. The Lone Wolf Syndrome article will still be sending people to Gerald's profile 6 months from now.
